Beware of Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks are one of the most common cybersecurity threats. Cybercriminals attempt to trick users into revealing personal information, such as passwords and financial details, by disguising themselves as trustworthy sources.

Common Signs of Phishing

  • Emails or messages that create a sense of urgency.
  • Suspicious links that redirect to fake websites.
  • Requests for sensitive information via email or text.
  • Unusual sender addresses or grammatical errors in messages.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never click on links from unknown sources.
  • Verify the sender's email address before responding.
  • Use security software to detect phishing attempts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication to secure your accounts.

What to Do If You Suspect a Phishing Attempt

If you receive a suspicious email or message, do not interact with it. Instead, report it to your email provider or IT department. If you accidentally enter your credentials on a fraudulent website, change your password immediately and enable additional security measures.
