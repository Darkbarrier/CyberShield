Secure Your Wi-Fi Network

Securing your home Wi-Fi network is essential to prevent unauthorized access and cyber threats. A poorly protected network can be exploited by hackers to steal data, install malware, or launch attacks on other systems.

Best Practices for Securing Wi-Fi

  • Use a strong and unique password for your Wi-Fi.
  • Enable WPA3 or WPA2 encryption for secure communication.
  • Change default router login credentials.
  • Disable remote access and unused services.
  • Regularly update your router firmware to patch vulnerabilities.

Why Wi-Fi Security Matters

Leaving your network open or using weak security settings makes it easier for cybercriminals to intercept data, compromise connected devices, and even launch cyberattacks from your network.

Additional Security Measures

  • Enable MAC address filtering to allow only trusted devices.
  • Turn off SSID broadcasting to make your network less visible.
  • Use a guest network for visitors to prevent access to main devices.
